Shinchan

Crayon Shin-chan first appeared in a Japanese weekly magazine called Weekly Manga Action on 11 April 1990. It started as a spin-off of the character Shinnosuke Nikaido of another series by Yoshito Usui, Darakuya Store Monogatari. The original version of the manga ended on September 11, 2009 due to Usui's death, as announced in a broadcast of the anime on October 16, 2009. Although the manga formally ended in February 5, 2010 with some new chapters in a new and last volume (50) due to new manuscripts found. It was announced on December 1, 2009 that a new manga would be published in the summer of 2010 by members of Usui's team under a new title: New Crayon Shin-chan.



On Monday April 13, 1992, the Crayon Shinchan anime started. After a month the audience had doubled to garner more than 10% ratings, and by the end of the year that rating had doubled again to more than 20%. To date more than 1000 episodes have been produced. In October 2004, the TV airings moved to Friday evening while in October 2019 the series moved to Saturday sometimes switching between morning and afternoon.
